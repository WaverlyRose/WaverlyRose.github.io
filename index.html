Waverly Rose Brim

Johns Hopkins Whiting School of Engineering

New York Metropolitan area

I’m an AI research engineer working at the intersection of foundation models, biological data, and surgical data science.

Research interests: multimodal foundation models, biomedical representation learning, computer vision for clinical decision systems, and computational pathology.

Current work spans a production LLM/RAG service for clinical decision support — Go-based orchestration, FastAPI/Pydantic, Postgres/pgvector retrieval on EKS, FHIR-like structured outputs with citation grounding; SCRUB, a computer vision system for surgical gloved-hand pose estimation; and an Apple Silicon–optimized port of MindEye2 for fMRI-to-image reconstruction.

Earlier work spans multimodal computational pathology and genomics in meningioma, real-time surgical skill assessment via computer vision, and genetically encoded voltage indicators engineered on a DARPA-funded neural engineering program. 20+ peer-reviewed publications, concentrated in systematic reviews of AI in neurooncology.
